Recycling Information & Education

What is E-Waste?

E-waste, or electronic waste, includes discarded electrical or electronic devices such as smartphones, laptops, and home appliances. These items contain hazardous materials like lead, mercury, and cadmium, which can harm the environment if not disposed of properly.

Impact of Recycling

  • Conserving Resources: Recycling helps recover valuable materials like metals and plastics, reducing the need for new raw materials.
  • Reducing Pollution: Proper disposal prevents toxic substances from contaminating soil and water sources.
  • Energy Efficiency: Recycling electronic components consumes less energy compared to producing new ones from raw materials.

Recycling Myths & Facts

  • Myth: E-waste recycling is not important.
    Fact: Electronics contain hazardous materials that can cause serious environmental damage if not recycled properly.
  • Myth: Recycling e-waste is expensive.
    Fact: Many organizations and governments offer free or low-cost recycling programs.
